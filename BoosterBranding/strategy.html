1. Mission, Vision & Values

Strategic Foundation

Booster Source is a long-term strategy to reposition Booster Juice as a transparent daily nourishment brand. It organizes wellness around visible nutritional value, clearer sourcing, and more responsible systems.

Mission

Move Booster from a legacy smoothie identity into a daily-use nourishment system that helps people choose faster and understand more.

Vision

Become Canada's most trusted and transparent daily nourishment brand.

Purpose

Help people make better daily food choices by showing what nourishes them, where it comes from, and how it is made more responsibly.

Values

Clarity

Make choices easier to understand and easier to navigate.

Responsibility

Align claims with better materials, operations, and measurable systems.

Nourishment

Focus on real daily needs: energy, protein, recovery, hydration, balance, and breakfast.

Transparency

Show what is inside each product and what sits behind it.

Community

Support the people and places connected to the Booster ecosystem.

Momentum

Fit active routines with speed, ease, and repeat relevance.

4. Daily Nourishment

Identity Implication

Daily Nourishment shifts Booster from an occasional smoothie stop toward a repeatable everyday routine built around clearer need states, faster decisions, and stronger daily relevance.

This pillar pushes the brand toward modular menu hierarchy, benefit-led navigation, and a more routine-oriented verbal and visual system.

Customer

Clearer choices, faster decision-making, and easier comparison across needs.

Store Teams

Simpler recommendation logic and stronger repeat-use mechanisms.

Digital Strategy

More routine-based ordering, bundling, and frequency growth.

Priority Moves & Metrics

Priority Moves

  • Organize menu by need states: Energy, Protein, Recovery, Focus, Balance, Breakfast.
  • Simplify add-ins, boosts, and decision pathways.
  • Build guided bundles and saved routine ordering.
  • Deploy an improved online ordering system.

Success Metrics

  • Repeat purchase rate.
  • Breakfast and afternoon occasion growth.
  • App reorder frequency.

5. Supply & Sourcing

Identity Implication

Supply & Sourcing makes ingredients, origins, and sourcing logic more visible, giving Booster a clearer and more credible product story.

Suggests provenance graphics, ingredient-forward information design, and a more human, traceable storytelling system.

Customer

Clearer ingredient understanding and stronger trust in product quality.

Growers & Suppliers

Greater visibility and accountability within the brand story.

Leadership

Better tools for explanation/storytelling, and more defensible transparency claims.

Priority Moves & Metrics

Priority Moves

  • Show ingredient origins and simplified source Journeys.
  • Explain proteins, boosters, and sweeteners in plain, understandable language.
  • Establish clearer sourcing standards and partner storytelling.

Success Metrics

  • Trust and transparency perception.
  • QR and sourcing content engagement rates.
  • Reduced customer confusion around ingredients and boosts.

6. Responsible Operations

Identity Implication

Responsible Operations aligns Booster's wellness promise with clearer packaging, disposal, and operational systems so responsibility feels visible and credible.

Requires restrained responsibility cues, honest material labeling, and clearer instructional graphics across packaging and store systems.

Customer

Clearer disposal behavior and more believable responsibility claims.

Store Teams

Simpler material systems and easier operational standards to maintain.

Disposal Partners

Better sorting, lower contamination, and clearer packaging logic.

Priority Moves & Metrics

Priority Moves

  • Simplify cup, lid, sleeve, and straw systems.
  • Improve disposal guidance at point of use.
  • Test reusable or return-based incentives.
  • Communicate measurable packaging and waste progress.

Success Metrics

  • Packaging weight reduction.
  • Reusable container participation rate.
  • Contamination reduction in disposal streams.

7. Social & Environmental Stewardship

Identity Implication

Social & Environmental Stewardship makes Booster's public role more visible by connecting the brand to communities, wellness access, and specific local contribution.

Points toward localized storytelling, clearer proof-based stewardship language, and a warmer but disciplined community-facing expression.

Communities

More visible local relevance and useful wellness participation.

Youth Partners

Clearer program alignment around nourishment and activity.

Franchises

Stronger public trust and more meaningful local activation tools.

Priority Moves & Metrics

Priority Moves

  • Build a clearer stewardship platform instead of scattered charity activity.
  • Support local youth wellness or nourishment initiatives.
  • Communicate where support goes with more specificity.
  • Localize community partnerships by region or store.

Success Metrics

  • Number and visibility of community partnerships.
  • Participation in local activations.
  • Trust, warmth, and brand relevance metrics.
