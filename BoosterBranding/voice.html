Voice

Clear, useful, transparent, and human.

1. Our Voice

Voice Principles

Booster speaks like a helpful daily-use brand: confident, plain-spoken, ingredient-aware, and practical. It should feel informed without becoming clinical, positive without becoming childish, and responsible without becoming preachy.

Clear

Use language that makes choices easier. Name the benefit, the ingredient, and the action.

Grounded

Avoid miracle claims and exaggerated energy language. Be specific instead.

Useful

Help people decide, compare, order, dispose, repeat, or learn.

Uplifting

Keep momentum and optimism, but never force cheerfulness or fake urgency.

Personality Range

What We Are

Clear, responsible, fresh, modern, functional, uplifting, transparent, and trustworthy.

Plain language Visible proof Routine logic Ingredient clarity

What To Avoid

Cluttered, childish, clinical, generic wellness, preachy, faux-eco, and claim-heavy writing.

No magic claims No hype loops No green fog

2. Language

Preferred Patterns

Benefit + Source

Use: "Sustained energy from matcha and mango." This makes the promise and proof visible in one line.

Need State + Action

Use: "Choose Recovery for protein, electrolytes, and post-workout routine." This helps people navigate quickly.

Material + Behavior

Use: "Empty, rinse, and recycle where accepted." Responsibility copy should tell people what to do next.

Vocabulary

Use

Nourishment, source, routine, visible nutrition, need state, recovery, balance, energy, focus, hydration, ingredients, material, recycle, progress, partner, local support.

Avoid

Magic, guilt-free, detox, cleanse, supercharged, miracle, ultimate health fix, eco-friendly without proof, all-natural as a blanket claim, and exaggerated lifestyle promises.

Headline Syntax

Formula

[Need] + [specific benefit] + [visible source]

Energy

Morning energy from matcha, mango, and vitamin C.

Recovery

Protein and electrolytes for the reset after movement.

Balance

Greens, fibre, and steady nourishment for the everyday gap.

3. Negative Examples & Re-writes

Practical Rewrites

Legacy copy often sounds either too excited, too vague, or too generic. The new voice fixes that by making each sentence more useful.

Don't

"Turbo-charge your day with our super yummy superfood blend!"

Do

Sustained energy from matcha, mango, and vitamin C.

Why: removes hype and gives a concrete source of benefit.

Don't

"Get your health fix with nature's magic."

Do

Your daily greens, blended with pineapple, spinach, and fibre.

Why: "health fix" and "magic" are vague. Ingredients and purpose are clearer.

Don't

Button: "Discover the magic"

Do

Button: "View ingredients"

Why: interface copy should predict the result of the tap.

Don't

"Eco-friendly cups for a greener tomorrow."

Do

Clear material labels help guests sort cups correctly where facilities accept them.

Why: responsibility language must name the system and avoid broad green claims.

Microcopy Rules

Ordering

Use action verbs: choose, add, remove, reorder, view, compare.

Nutrition

Prefer exact amounts and ingredient sources over benefit-only claims.

Errors

State what happened and what to do next. Keep Boo supportive, not jokey.

Sustainability

Name materials, acceptance limits, and next steps. Do not overstate impact.

Voice exists to make the system easier to understand, not louder.
